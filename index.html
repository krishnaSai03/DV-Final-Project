GNI per capita is the total income earned by a country's residents and businesses, including any income from abroad, divided by the population of the country. It reflects the average income of a country's citizens, converted to US dollars using the World Bank method.

Life expectancy is the average number of years a newborn is expected to live if current mortality rates continue to apply. It's a widely used indicator of health.

The scatter plot is generated using the merged CSV file containing GNI and life expectancy data. Each point represents a country, and detailed information is displayed when hovering over each point. Countries with higher GNI per capita might afford better healthcare systems, more extensive public health measures, and higher-quality nutrition, all of which contribute to longer life expectancies.

The line graph displays the Gross National Income (GNI) per capita over time for selected country, providing insights into economic growth trends.

The choropleth map shows GNI per capita across different countries with respective to the Year. Different shades of color indicate the GNI per capita range, with darker colors representing higher GNI per capita. The user can input a year to view the economic status of countries for that particular year.
